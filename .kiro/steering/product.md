# FiqhApp — Product Overview

FiqhApp delivers precise, authoritative Islamic jurisprudence (Fiqh) rulings through a mobile interface. It bridges classical legal texts and modern UX, providing practitioners with reliable guidance based on the four Sunni schools of thought (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali).

## Core Concept: "Peaceful Scholar"

The app uses a layered information architecture:
- **Level 1 — Quick Answer**: `short_rule` gives a concise ruling at a glance.
- **Level 2 — The How-To**: `actionable_steps` provide step-by-step practical guidance.
- **Level 3 — Evidence & Heart**: `quran_hadith_evidence` and `spiritual_wisdom` offer scriptural backing and contextual insight.

## Key User Flows

- **Home (Situation Finder)**: Users pick a life situation (Daily Essentials, Traveling, Sick, Business) and see relevant rulings filtered and grouped by ritual phase.
- **Explore (Classical Libraries)**: Hierarchical drill-down — Module → Topic → Chapter Guide (article-style view with conditions, method steps, evidence, and references).
- **Bookmarks**: Saved rulings for quick access.
- **Settings**: Madhhab (school of thought) preference that globally filters all rulings fetched from the database.

## Data Coverage

27 topics across 4 schools of thought, organized into these modules:
- **Purification**: Wudu, Ghusl, Tayammum, Khuffs, Menstruation/Nifas, Removing Filth
- **Prayer**: Salah (times, conditions, pillars, obligations, recommended acts, invalidators, forgetfulness, voluntary, congregational, Friday, traveler's, sick, funeral)
- **Fasting**: Sawm
- **Charity**: Zakat, Zakat al-Fitr
- **Pilgrimage**: Hajj, Umrah, Sacrifice (Udhiyah)

## UX Persona: The Empathetic Mentor

All content in the app speaks directly to the user in second person ("you"). The tone is:
- Warm, dignified modern English
- No archaic words (incumbent, eschew, thereof)
- No slang
- Concessions framed as mercies
- Boundaries framed as gentle reminders, not punishments
- Spiritual wisdom focused on inner peace and connection to the Divine

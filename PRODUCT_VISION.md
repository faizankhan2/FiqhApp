# FiqhApp: Product Vision & North Star

## 1. Core Philosophy: The 'Peaceful Scholar'
Our guiding aesthetic is the **'Peaceful Scholar'**. We are moving away from the cold, clinical feel of a 'stern legal textbook' towards a **'gentle lifestyle friend.'** The app should feel like a wise, compassionate mentor accompanying the user through their daily life, offering guidance that is both authoritative and accessible.

## 2. Information Architecture
### Contextual Categorization
We prioritize **Life Situations** over traditional academic structures. While classical 'Kitab' chapters are preserved in metadata, the primary user journey is organized by real-world contexts:
- *“I am traveling”*
- *“I am sick”*
- *“I am at work”*
- *“I am preparing for prayer”*

### The Main/Other Toggle
To prevent cognitive overload, we enforce a strict hierarchy:
- **Daily Essentials**: High-frequency rulings (Salah, Wudu, Fasting) are front-and-center.
- **Specialized Fiqh**: Complex topics (Inheritance, Business, Waqf) are kept secondary—accessible through deep-search or specific modules, but never cluttering the daily experience.

## 3. The 3-Level Depth Rule
Every ruling must be consumed according to the user's immediate need for detail:

1.  **Level 1: The Quick Answer**
    - **Source**: `short_rule`
    - **UX**: A 1-sentence summary at the top of the card for instant clarity.
2.  **Level 2: The How-To**
    - **Source**: `actionable_steps`
    - **UX**: A step-by-step practical checklist showing exactly *how* to implement the ruling.
3.  **Level 3: The Evidence & Heart**
    - **Source**: `quran_hadith_evidence` and `spiritual_wisdom`
    - **UX**: Deep-dive content for the student of knowledge, providing scriptural backing and the spiritual 'Why.'

## 4. Visual Guidelines
- **Palette**: Soft pastels (Sage, Cream, Dusty Blue). Avoid high-contrast blacks and harsh primaries.
- **Shape**: Generous rounded corners (16pt+) for a soft, approachable feel.
- **Iconography**: Minimalist, thin-line illustrations (Feather Icons/Custom).
- **Typography**: Clean, modern sans-serif hierarchy (e.g., Inter or System Sans) to maximize legibility without academic pretension.

## 5. Tone of Voice
- **Encouraging**: Use "We invite you to..." rather than "You must...".
- **Calm**: Short, clear sentences. No exclamation marks or alarmist language.
- **Ease in the Deen**: Always highlight the *rukhsa* (concessions) and ease where the Shari'ah provides them.
